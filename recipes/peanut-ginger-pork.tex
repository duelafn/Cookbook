
\begin{recipe}{Peanut Ginger Pork}
  \rating    4
  \nutrition 3
  \health    3
  \workload{medium}
  \time{60}
  \yield{}
  \servings{4}
  \source{Dean}
  \maketitle
  \photo{peanut-ginger-pork}

  \begin{ingredients2}
    \threequarter \c & stock\\
    3 \T & dashi\\
    6 \T & soy sauce\\
    2 \T & molasses\\
    2 \t & sesame oil\\
    2 \T & rice vinegar\\
    2 \T & peanut butter\\
    1 \T & lime\\
    4 & cloves garlic\\
    1 \t & sriracha\\
    \fourth \c & thai chili sauce\\
    $3+$ thumbs & ginger\\
    1\half \lb & ground pork\\
    8 \oz & shelled edamame\\
    8 \oz & broccoli\\
    12 \oz & ramen noodles
  \end{ingredients2}

  This is similar to \reciperef{Pork Noodle Stir-Fry} with different spicing. This is
  more spicy and should lean into the ginger. It also relies on sesame oil rather
  than Chinese five spice for its base notes.

  Prepare as above.
\end{recipe}
